# ADR-004: Truth-Status Awareness

Status: Accepted

## Decision

Every claim shall carry one of four truth-status labels:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `fact` | stated as objective truth in the source |
| `belief` | held as true by a character or faction, not the narrator |
| `interpretation` | an inference drawn from evidence, not directly stated |
| `rumor` | reported as unverified or hearsay within the source |

## Why

Source texts contain a mix of objective narrative statements and subjective or unreliable ones. Conflating them corrupts retrieval quality and misleads answers. Distinguishing truth status preserves the epistemic structure of the lore.

## Implications

- Extraction prompts must elicit a truth-status label for every claim.
- Retrieval queries may filter or weight results by status (e.g., exclude `rumor` from authoritative answers).
- The four labels are the minimum set for the pilot. Additional labels may be
  added later with a new ADR.
- A claim's truth status reflects the source text's framing, not the extractor's judgment.
