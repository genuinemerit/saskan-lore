# ADR-003: Claims as First-Class Records

Status: Accepted

## Decision

All extracted knowledge is stored as discrete claims, each linked to a source span in a specific chunk. Claims are the primary unit of retrieval and evaluation. Summaries are secondary.

## Why

Claims are traceable, testable, and comparable. Storing only summaries or tags would obscure provenance and make contradiction analysis impossible. A claim tied to a source span can be verified against the original text.

## Implications

- Every claim record must include: claim text, source span (verbatim excerpt), document ID, and chunk ID.
- Retrieval queries target claims, not summaries.
- Evaluation questions are written against claims.
- Summaries may be stored but are not used for retrieval in the pilot.
