# ADR-007: No Lore Expansion During Extraction

Status: Accepted

## Decision

The system shall not introduce new lore, fill narrative gaps, or alter existing material during extraction or any processing stage. All enrichment must be structural — metadata, labels, relationships — never narrative.

## Rules

- Extract only what is explicitly stated or directly and unambiguously implied in the source.
- Do not infer details that would add new canon.
- Do not complete, extend, or fill gaps in the source material.
- Do not rewrite or paraphrase source text in claim records; preserve the original wording in source spans.

## Why

Invented or inferred content, even plausible content, corrupts the integrity of the lore corpus and is not recoverable once loaded. The system's value depends entirely on its faithfulness to the source.

## Implications

- LLM extraction prompts must explicitly instruct the model not to invent or infer beyond the source.
- Human review (Stage 4) is the control point for catching any expansion that slipped through.
- If a gap in the lore is identified during extraction, it is recorded as a gap, not filled.
